- Dealership groups ($10M–$200M+ annual volume).

## Personas (page S8)

| Role | Pain | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| VP / Director Finance or Ops | Interchange erodes margin on every $1M processed. | Cut $30K out of every $1M without disrupting checkout. |
| Digital / IT Director | DMS handles payment but generates zero loyalty data. | Own the customer-data layer the DMS doesn't capture. |
| CX Manager | Loyalty programs benefit Visa, not the dealership. | Reward program in your dealership's brand, paying $1=$1. |
| Dealership Principal | Margins shrinking, no leverage on payment processing. | A structural cost reduction competitors don't have. |

## Pain (use the merchant's language)

- 1.5–3.5% interchange on every transaction.
- No post-sale visibility once the deal is signed.
- DMS handles the payment but doesn't generate loyalty or repeat-purchase data.
- High-ticket transactions ($30K–$80K vehicles) make interchange brutal at scale.
- F&I is the most profitable line on the floor — and it's leaking margin to card networks.

## Anti-patterns (don't say this)

- "Revolutionize your F&I process." (cliché)
- "Empower your dealership with cutting-edge fintech." (consumer marketing)
- "We help dealerships save money." (vague + hedged)
- Anything that frames Visa as a partner.

## Tone notes specific to this vertical

Automotive decision-makers are skeptical, numbers-driven, and have heard every fintech pitch. The win comes from:
1. Specific dollar figures in the first sentence.
2. F&I-process fluency (you sound like you've stood at the desk).
3. Operator-to-operator tone, not vendor-to-buyer.
