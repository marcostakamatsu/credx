# Audience — Entertainment

> Used for `copy/entertainment.md`. Read before drafting any section on that page.

## Targets

- Venue operators (arenas, theatres, concert halls).
- Ticketing platforms (Power Up Sports is a named target).
- Indoor entertainment franchises (indoor golf, escape rooms, FECs / Family Entertainment Centers).

## Personas (page S8)

| Role | Pain | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| VP Operations / GM | High-volume, low-margin transactions. Interchange eats event-night P&L. | Cut interchange to a sixth, keep loyalty mechanism. |
| CMO | Marketing spend goes to acquisition; retention is owned by the card. | Retention mechanism in your venue's brand, not Visa's. |
| Head of Digital / Tech | No payment stack that captures repeat-visit data. | First-party data on every visit, your platform. |
| Franchise Owner / Regional Director | Multi-location loyalty is fragmented; nothing rolls up. | One CredX account, every venue branded, consolidated reporting. |

## Pain (use the merchant's language)

- High-volume, low-margin transactions every event night.
- Captive audience with no merchant-owned repeat-visit mechanism.
- Loyalty owned by the card (Visa, Amex), not the venue.
- Concession transactions are small and frequent — interchange compounds fast.
- No first-party signal on what brings a guest back.

## Volume language

- **Per event night:** $60,000 in interchange leaving the building (an NHL game scale — sourced from `plan/01.Brand-Analysis.md` §Stats).
- **Default calculator value:** $2,000,000/month (event-night sized).
- Talk in *nights* and *events* — not annualized abstract figures.

## Vertical-specific examples (S4 step examples, S7 storyboard)

- **Concession line:** doors open, guests queue. Currently every scan is 3% out the door.
- **QR at the till:** guest scans; Onus.ai opens a venue-branded credit line in 6 minutes.
- **Wallet drop:** $1=$1 rewards land in the guest's wallet under your venue's brand.
- **Repeat visit:** next event, reward pulls them back. The loyalty is yours, not Visa's.

## Vertical-specific FAQ (S11 extras)

- Multi-venue operators (one master CredX account, individual venue branding, consolidated reporting).
- Event-day spikes (QR-based checkout, no terminal queue, Onus.ai approvals at peak load).

## Anti-patterns (don't say this)

- "Engage your fans / guests with personalized rewards." (consumer marketing)
- "Boost your concession revenue." (vague + buzzword)
- "Modernize your venue's payment experience." (generic)
- "Visa partner network." (Visa is the system being disrupted — never frame as partner here)
- Anything that hints at a single-venue / single-team scope when the audience runs multi-location operations.

## Tone notes specific to this vertical

Venue operators live in event-night urgency: peak-load handling, queue throughput, real-time settlement, beer-and-burger economics. The win comes from:
1. Numbers translated into *per-night* terms, not abstract percentages.
2. Operational fluency (you sound like you've been backstage at a sold-out show).
3. Acknowledging the captive-audience reality without sounding extractive.
