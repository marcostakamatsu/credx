# Audience — Sports

> Used for `copy/sports.md`. Read before drafting any section on that page.

## Targets

- Pro teams & leagues (NHL, CFL, MLS, WHL).
- Stadium / arena operators.
- Merch & concession operators.
- League administrative bodies.

## Personas (page S8)

| Role | Pain | Outcome |
|---|---|---|
| CRO / VP Business Development | $60K+ per game night leaves the building on interchange. | Cut to a sixth; loyalty asset retained. |
| VP Fan Experience | Most loyal customers on earth earn rewards for Visa, not the team. | Rewards in team currency, paying $1=$1 — a true fan asset. |
| Head of Partnerships | Card-network rails block deeper sponsor monetization. | First-party credit + loyalty you can offer sponsors directly. |
| CFO / VP Finance | Payment processing is a fixed cost with no leverage. | Structural shift — concession revenue keeps more of every dollar. |

## Pain (use the team's language)

- The most loyal fans on earth — and the loyalty accrues to Visa, not the team.
- $60K+ per game night in interchange leaves the arena.
- Sponsorship monetization is capped by what card networks allow.
- Fan-experience teams can't tie revenue to retention because the data layer is owned by the card.
- Concession + merch + parking — three separate revenue lines, all bleeding interchange.

## Volume language

- **Per game night:** $60K+ in interchange (from Brand Analysis Stats Inventory).
- **Default calculator value:** $3,000,000/month (event volume on a big night).
- **Per-fan credit line:** $5,000 white-labelled credit (the "Flames Bucks" / "Oilers Credit" example).
- Talk in *nights*, *seasons*, *fanbases* — not abstract MRR.

## Demo story (named in Brand Analysis)

**Calgary Flames example** — fan at concession opens a $5,000 white-labelled credit line ("Flames Bucks") at POS; viral revenue share with fans.

The "$5,000 Flames Bucks" framing is the page's emotional core. Use it. Reuse it. Make it the spine of the storyboard (S7).

## Vertical-specific examples (S4 step examples, S7 storyboard)

- **Pre-game:** fans arrive in team colors. Currently every scan = 3% to Visa.
- **First beer at the concession:** QR scan opens $5,000 credit in your team's brand.
- **Reward in team currency:** $1 spent = $1 earned in Flames Bucks / Oilers Credit / [team-branded reward].
- **Next home game:** reward pulls them back. Relationship is yours, interchange savings are yours.

## Vertical-specific FAQ (S11 extras)

- League-wide rollouts (each team operates its own branded credit + loyalty layer; shared infrastructure, distinct currency per team).
- Fan use outside the venue (yes — once a fan has a team-branded credit line, it works at any participating merchant; the team brand follows them).

## Anti-patterns (don't say this)

- "Engage your fans like never before." (cliché)
- "Drive incremental concession revenue." (vague, marketing-speak)
- "Loyalty platform for sports brands." (commoditizes the offer)
- Anything that frames the fan as a transaction rather than a relationship.
- Naming a team without explicit public-use approval.

## Tone notes specific to this vertical

Sports operators carry tribal language: team currency, fan tribes, season-ticket cohorts, rival-team rivalry. The win comes from:
1. Treating loyalty as emotional, not transactional.
2. Using *team brand* not *team name* unless approved (so anyone reading it from any team identifies).
3. Numbers framed *per game night* — visceral, not annualized.
4. Acknowledging fans as the asset; Visa as the parasite.
