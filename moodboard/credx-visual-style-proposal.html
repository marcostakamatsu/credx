CredX Brand System 01. Visual Concept

Quantified Manifesto.

The page should feel like a financial proof point and a movement at the same time. Numbers do the selling. Restraint creates trust.

Support Principles 01. Visual Concept

Brex clarity

Lead with the business number. Make savings, cost, speed, and ownership visible within the first scan.

Mercury restraint

Warm light surfaces, editorial typography, fine rules, spacious layouts, and interface mockups instead of loud stock photography.

Vertical flexibility

The system flexes across Automotive, Entertainment, Sports, and POS with one accent, one audience hook, and one proof-led story.

CredX Identity 02. Concept

Visual Identity

Real credit. Real savings. Merchant-owned loyalty.

Structural: CredX is infrastructure, not a feature add-on.

Measured: Every major section earns its place with a number.

Controlled: Dark Blue, white, and one vertical accent keep the system sharp.

Palette 03. Colors

Dark Blue base. One accent per vertical.

Use no more than three brand colors in a single asset, plus white. The parent brand stays calm; vertical accents create page-level distinction.

Voice Tone 04. Voice Tone

Bold. Trustworthy. Empowering.

Senior B2B decision-maker tone. Founder-driven, direct, and ruthlessly simple.

Lead with proof: "$30,000 saved per $1M" before abstract benefits.
Frame the movement: "Software should pay for people, not people for software."
Own the contrast: CredX versus legacy card-network economics, without sounding political or reckless.
Keep it scan-ready: short sections, concrete claims, no hierarchy-less sales-deck density.
Avoid: consumer fintech flash, apology language, vague innovation claims, and over-explaining.
Typography 05. Typography

Fraunces for display.

Imagery 06. Imagery

Editorial product storytelling.

Use: line illustrations, dashboard mockups, smartphone checkout, merchant operations, lender/partner flows, and retention reporting.

Style: warm cream backgrounds, dark navy line work, one brand accent on the most important detail.

Avoid: card-network logos, real partner logos without approval, glossy AI realism, clutter, consumer-app neon, generic handshakes.

Need: public-use partner logos, real team headshots, and vertical-specific photography can replace placeholders later.
